Malaysia & Global VR Solutions Review

Globally, technologies like **HTC Viverse** and **Second Life** highlight two major design paradigms in user engagement. Viverse prioritizes browser-accessible **WebXR frameworks**, making virtual worlds lightweight and friction-free across desktop and mobile devices. Second Life relies heavily on heavy local clients with vast persistent asset databases, producing highly loyal but highly-restricted localized desktop user patterns.

Global Architectural Limits

High-fidelity spatial engines typically load multi-gigabyte models directly into memory. In developing regions, this creates a major barrier, preventing users without high-end hardware or direct fiber connections from participating.

Malaysian Ecosystem Gaps

Local industrial efforts focus heavily on enterprise simulations or basic 360-degree real-estate tours. There is a lack of lightweight consumer platforms merging cultural identity, remote collaboration, and social commerce.

architecture

Stanford University Design Thinking

Unlike traditional application designs built for two-dimensional screens, **spatial user environments** must be modeled using design iterations centered deeply on bodily presence, field-of-view limits, and visual comfort. We apply the **Stanford Design Thinking Framework** to systematically map human physical capabilities to WebXR requirements.

1

Step 1: Empathize (Addressing Hardware Constraints)

We evaluated local device limitations, focusing specifically on unstable cellular network quality and high thermal loads generated on standard mid-tier smartphones.

2

Step 2: Define (Targeting Latency & Accessibility)

Rather than viewing the challenge as a purely visual aesthetic goal, the design requirements were targeted to solve data latency, asset processing complexity, and virtual motion sickness.

3

Step 3: Ideate (Designing Spatial UI Anchors)

The team conceived localized UI structures, replacing standard flat menus with responsive interaction anchors, such as the golden 'Wau' kite floating action button.

4

Step 4: Prototype (Generating Multi-Screen Views)

We compiled all physical layout interactions, asset customizers, timeline mechanics, and social chat interfaces into the modular simulator layout on the right.

5

Step 5: Test (Evaluating Interactive Prototypes)

We monitor real-world actions, testing layout elements, asset loading sequences, and network variables to verify smooth frame-rates and intuitive navigation.
